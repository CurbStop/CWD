Mining

Background

The mining industry in Mozambique is one of the most promising. In recent years huge deposits of coal have been found which could make it one of the world's largest players. It is projected that two of the fastest developing economies, China and India will buy the bulk of the mined commodity.

Other commodities include gold, aluminum, and a variety of metals such as tantalum and beryllium. Precious stones such as diamonds and rubies are also being mined. The Government of Mozambique allocates licenses which determine the area within which companies have rights to mine This has attracted the attention of international firms who are keen on developing the potential of the country.

In order to facilitate this process, the Government is working hard to simplify regulation. One obstacle is the lack of infrastructure in the areas where significant activity is taking place. Since Mozambique does not have the funds to develop infrastructure, it has turned to the mining companies to do this.

Development Challenges

Mozambique while having enormous potential will have to overcome the following limitation before it can narrow the gap it has with much more advanced economies also harnessing the capacity of coal:

  • The infrastructure required to transport the coal from the concession area to the closest port is not in place and therefore has to be constructed
  • Land regulations complicate the resettlement of populations and can further add to the cost of doing business
  • High automation levels in modern mining techniques means that there is declining need for labour which may not be compatible with the socio-political environment of the country
  • A stable political environment is necessary for business confidence and consequently foreign investment, this is an area which will be watched over closely
